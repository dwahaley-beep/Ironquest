About

What's true of most stories is also true of mine — you're meeting me on the other side of a lot of figuring out, and it probably looks cleaner from here than it felt from inside it.

None of this has been graceful.

I was shy for most of my life — so quiet that nobody ever noticed I was struggling. I've spent years as an adult working hard to understand myself, to find my voice, and to trust that it matters. I certainly didn't do this work alone — my husband Adam has been one of the most important mirrors of my life, reflecting me back to myself through all of it. I believe we heal through relationship, not before it.

Going for it scared isn't a cute phrase for me — it's truly how I do most things.

When I was young, I lost my mom. It left me with a tender, grieving heart — the kind that makes you pay attention to what people are carrying, to what they need, and to what they're not saying out loud.

[ a photo from early life or home ]

In 9th grade, I went to Honduras with a group of friends and family. I didn't know what to expect, but what I found was joy, connection, community, presence — people living with so much despite having very little materially. I wanted that, too.

To be clear — joy and connection are not consolation prizes for poverty. Everyone deserves full bellies, safety, and dignity — and a life with both material security and community. What I witnessed wasn't an argument for having less. It was a reminder of what we're all actually hungry for.

So I started learning Spanish — not to be impressive, but to actually connect.

That was the beginning of a long love affair with language — and with the belief that everyone deserves to be understood in their own words.

I spent the next fifteen years inside classrooms and communities around the world — teaching immigrant and refugee students in Nashville, student teaching in Guatemala, coaching teachers, and even earning a Fulbright grant that took me to Bogotá, Colombia to work in bilingual education in public schools.

I loved the work and the people — I still do. But eventually something shifted — the feeling of being a cog in a system that wasn't always serving the people it was supposed to. So I stepped back.

[ teaching, travel, or transitional moment ]

Yoga found me in that in-between space. The practices and rituals it offered brought me back to myself — not a fixed or improved version, but the whole one. The one where nothing is wrong, everything belongs, and I am exactly who the world needs me to be.

And somehow, teaching it found me, too. It gave me something the classroom never quite could — the ability to work with the whole person, not just in an academic setting.

Slowly something has started to take shape that feels more like me — work that is mine to create, on my own terms. Now I'm building something rooted in the same belief I've carried since that very first trip to Honduras:

That everyone already belongs, just as we are. We just sometimes need someone to remind us.

02 — Language School Coming Soon

Bilingual Language School

Together — that's the whole idea.

A bilingual English and Spanish learning community built for people who are curious, ready, and maybe a little intimidated about where to start.

That's okay. That's actually the perfect place to start.

We serve English language learners, Spanish learners, and heritage speakers reconnecting with a language that has always been theirs — just waiting to be reclaimed.

Group classes are designed to be small and community-centered. One-on-one lessons are also available — for those who want a more personalized pace.

Pricing is sliding scale — structured around what you can genuinely afford, because language and belonging shouldn't have a price tag that keeps people out.

04 — Immersion Trips

Language Immersion Trips

The best way to learn a language is to live inside it for a while.

Every year we lead small group immersion trips to Spanish-speaking countries — exploring cities, connecting with local communities, and practicing language in the most authentic way possible: real conversations, real places, real life.

Past destinations include Mexico, Guatemala, and Colombia.
Next up: Peru in 2027.

Trips are roughly 10 days and open to anyone — whether you're a confident Spanish speaker looking to go deeper or a beginner who learns best by jumping in.
